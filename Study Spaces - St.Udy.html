Whether you are looking for a quiet place to concentrate, a cafe with delicious tumeric lattes, or a place to relax and work on a group project St.udy has got you covered!

Check out our study spaces below...



Main Library

The Main Library is the place to go if you want access to the largest amount of resources; it offers both silent study spaces, private study rooms to conduct group work, and social areas. It also holds the most resources in terms of books, journals, videos, and periodicals out of any other building in St Andrews; and the Library Staff are always more than happy to help you find a particular source for your project.

Website: St Andrews University Main Library


King James Library

If you are looking for a more aesthetically pleasing place to revise, with natural lighting you should check out the King James Library. However, be sure to get here early as seats fill up quickly, and people often stay all day.

Website: King James Library

JF Allen Library

Located on the North Haugh, this is the perfect place to go if you are inbetween classes and do not have enough time to walk all the way back to town.

Website: JF Allen

The Richardson Research Library at Martyrs Kirk

Not many know about the library directly across from St Salvador's quad which is held in an old church, with stained glass lighting, and heaters under the desks. The Richardson Research Library at Martyrs Kirk is the perfect place for Postgrads and Masters students to hit the books. Unfortunately for undergraduates you can only get access into this library with a Postgrad/Masters Matriculation card.

Website: The Richardson Research Library at Martyrs Kirk

Pret A Manger

Pret A Manger is the perfect place to go in the mornings from 7am-11am to get some work done in a relaxing setting. Their tumeric latte is also the perfect drink to fight off the inflammation from the weekend, and they also offer a selection of fresh food options to fuel your brain.

Website: Pret A Manger - St Andrews

Rectors

Rectors is a great place for a casual meet up or to discuss a group project. It is well lit with natural sunlight streaming through the floor to ceiling glass windows. They also offer a selection of food and drinks.

Website: Rectors

Old Union Cafe

The Old Union Cafe is a cosy place to grab a mid-day coffee and get some work done, especially if the library is too crowded, as it is right around the corner.

Website: Old Union Cafe

Bell Pettigrew Museum

This Museum located in the Bute Building has a plethora of taxidermic animals to admire, and there are also tables in the large open museum lobby for students to work. An inspiring place for Biologist to work, and never very busy, so when revision hits and the library is full at 12pm, this is a great study space to check out!

Website: Bell Pettigrew Museum
